Cumulative Reflections

1 Sep, 2016 · Read in about 2 min · (370 Words)

My cumulative experience at Iowa State University has strongly prepared me for a career in software engineering. My class experience has provided me with the problem solving skills to impact engineering solutions. Classes such as Project Management and Software Architecture have shaped my abilities to design systems and develop them efficiently. While working on team projects I have utilized technical communication skills. Side projects as well as the freedom of project choice in several classes have allowed me to pursue the learning of new technologies. Through those, I’ve been able to apply my knowledge learned in class while also broadening my software development skills. This has developed my confidence in experimenting with new technologies in the future.

My time at Iowa State has prepared me for the professional engineering community. I have been introduced to contemporary issues through guest lecturers and elective courses. In my project management class, we had a guest from the Department of Defense speak to us about modern software security concerns. In my History and Religions classes we learned about other less technical issues around the world. In my Senior Design course, we have spent time discussing professional and ethical responsibilities, and discussed some modern examples, such as a Ford car recall and how the engineers handled it.

My software engineering education has provided me with the skills to go beyond what was learned in the classroom to learn more concepts independently. In my Software Testing class, as well as my Computer Graphics course, we were required to independently study and report on an advanced topic in the field. For each course, I analyzed profoessional journal papers written by experts in the field to acquire more understanding on my topic. I have also had to read papers and discuss with experts as a part of my Undergraduate Research Experience. My collaboration with other students in my research work has proven critical to my success. Journal papers contain technical information that has helped me understand advanced concepts. My discussions with other students have helped me to think of better ways of implementing solutions. These experiences have shown me that lifelong learning is key to a successful career in software engineering, as there are always new problems arising.
