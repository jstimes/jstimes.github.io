Oranges To Oranges

1 May, 2015 · Read in about 2 min · (222 Words)

A semester long group project, in which we developed an Android game based off of the popular card game Apples-to-Apples. Users could start games to play with other friends who also had the app, and create/use custom made cards and decks.

Our project consisted of an Android application and an Apache server running PHP. Users could sign into the app and look at games they were currently playing with other friends as well as look at previous games. We had matchmaking such that players could invite their friends (using Facebook API integration) or join a game with random players. The in-game experience was intuitive in that users could look through the cards in their hand, and drag one out to the center to play a card. Additionally, an interesting feature of the app was the ability for users to create their own cards and build custom decks that could be shared with others.

My role on the project was Team Lead. I was responsible for organizing and running our meetings, and keeping track of what things we still had to complete and how to prioritize them. I worked on the client app as well as the server code.

The code for this project was originally hosted on a school-provided repository server. If there is interest, I can provide the code upon request.
